Error Tracking Tool at HelloFresh

Research Scoping UX UI

GOAL
How might we decrease filing time of customer complaints for customer care agents while simultaneously decreasing and creating consistency of complaint compensations?

ROLE
I was sole product designer on the Customer Care team. My (role) consisted of scoping out the problem, conducting user research, meeting with stakeholders, developing end to end user flows, wire framing ideas, creating visual design from scratch, and conducting user testing.

PROBLEM
CC agents have constant frustration around adding accurate compensation for the relevant complaint and overall filing/solving customer complaints in an efficient (quick) amount of time.

USER SHADOWING + INTERVIEWS
I shadowed and conducted user interviews with 5 U.S. CC Agents in the Newark, New Jersey office to validate stakeholder assumptions with the user’s themselves.




STAKEHOLDER INTERVIEWS
I talked to stakeholders to build a rapport with them to build trust and to better understand what they wanted out of this project and did my best to align their wants and needs with the user needs and frustrations.



USER CONCEPT TESTING
While visiting the Newark office, I treated my visit like a mini 8 day design sprint. So at the end of my visit I user tested a very low fidelity prototype I build that was aiming to gather feedback from the users and make sure I was on track with solving their most pressing paint-points while still meeting business objectives. After I tested the prototype I presented the results to the stakeholders in the Newark office.

LOW FIDELITY WIREFRAMES
This stage is all about quantity over quality. To explore ideas super quickly and not get caught up in judgement or limitations. MVP user flows I focused on were: Happy Path Late Delivery, Happy Path Bad Ingredient, Override Compensation, and File 2 Errors at Once (I landed on these as the MVP flows due to the alignment between user problems and business problems and was able to add even more user flows to the MVP)





MID FIDELITY WIREFRAMES
As the concept solidified more and more (due to dev limitations, time restrictions, and through constant communication with users, stakeholders, and the CC Product team, I arrived with higher fidelity wireframes. I like to think of it like slowly carving away at a sculpture. At this point you would be starting to carve out more defining features.
