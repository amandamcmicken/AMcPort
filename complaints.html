Error Tracking Tool at HelloFresh

Research Scoping UX UI

GOAL
How might we decrease filing time of customer complaints for cc agents and create consistency of customer compensations?

ROLE
I was the sole product designer on the Customer Care team. My role consisted of scoping out the problem, conducting user research, meeting with stakeholders, developing end-to-end user flows, wire framing ideas, creating visual design from scratch, and conducting user testing.

PROBLEM SCOPING
Now that the initial research is complete for users (as well as on the business objective side), I worked with the PO and development team to scope out the main problem themes.
Provide Automatic Compensation

We found manual compensation was a huge issue on both the business and cc agent side. Compensations for the same complaint would vary greatly from cc agent to cc agent, inevitably costing HelloFresh too much money.

Decrease Filing Time of Customer Complaint

Due to poor UX + UI, and specifically the manual nature of the the old tool, cc agent's took too much time to file a customer complaint. Filing a complaint was taking ~3 minutes.

LOW FIDELITY WIREFRAMES
This stage is all about quantity over quality. I was able to explore ideas super quickly and not get caught up in judgement or limitations. MVP user flows I specifically focused on were: Happy Path for Late Delivery, Happy Path for Bad Ingredient, Overriding Compensation, and Filing 2 Errors at Once (Due to user and business alignment).




snapshot of some user testing results from A/B testing error categories


MID FIDELITY WIREFRAMES
As the concept solidified more and more, through constant communication with users, stakeholders, and the CC Product team, I arrived with higher fidelity wireframes where hierarchy was being established. I like to think of this part of the process as slowly carving a marble sculpture. At this point one would be starting to carve out the more defining features.





USER CONCEPT TESTING
While at the Newark office, I treated my visit like a design sprint. By the end of the trip, I had user tested a couple low fidelity prototypes that aimed to gather feedback from the users on the basic UX flow and common user scenarios. This was to make sure I was on track with solving their most pressing pain-points while still meeting business objectives. After I tested the prototypes, I presented the results to the U.S. stakeholders to make sure we were all aligned before moving forward with ideation.

FINAL MVP DESIGNS

Within 2 months the Customer Care Complaints Tool MVP was released! This tool solves the cc agent’s need to file customer complaints quicker in order to achieve more than 8 complaints in an hour and completely eliminate frustrations surrounding customer compensation! The final deliverables consisted of research assets, as-is and to-be scenario’s, user flows, high-fidelity wireframes that were achieved through collaboration with the users, stakeholders, PO, and development team!








In tandem with this project, myself and Polina, the other SCM product designer, created the SCM pattern library with along with 3 front-end developers from the SCM team!
