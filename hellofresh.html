HelloFresh Pair Cooking | Personal Project

UX | UI | Research

GOAL
Eliminate frustration of cooking recipes with others.

ROLE
I was responsible for every element of the design process in this week long capsule-sized personal project, including user research, paper prototyping, testing, wireframing, and hign-fidelity designs.

PROBLEM
HelloFresh customers struggle to enjoy the experience of cooking recipes with another person i.e. a spouse, friend, roommate, etc. because the recipes are designed with only 1 cook in mind.


PROTOTYPE LINK

RESEARCH
Because my initial task was to "add a new feature or redesign an existing feature that you think would increase user engagement with the HelloFresh app", I first had to do some desktop and user research to narrow the project scope. Research helped me narrow down my problem scope to focus on the painpoints surrounding the cooking process, specifically cooking with others.




PERSONAS
Because of the short timeline, I created a persona through general research and limited user research (3 people), but hey it is user research nonetheless!

Lauren the Lover of Shared Experiences

29 years old, has an active schedule, values convenience and shared experiences like cooking with her husband




AS - IS SCENARIO
Lauren explained how she currently cooks HelloFresh meals with her husband. She reads through all of the steps up front, and her and Jim have to constantly re-read the intructions to remember who is doing what part.



PAINPOINTS
Because the design ask was rather broad, I did further investigation and narrowed down the scope of the problem. From the research I did shown above, I arrived at these painpoints that hinder the value proposition which is to provide an enjoyable shared cooking experience.
Adaptability

Lauren isn't able to enjoy cooking with others because the recipe instructions aren't optimized for 2 cooks.

Time

Lauren and Jim end up taking longer than the ETA given to cook a recipe, because the instructions aren't designed with more than 1 cook in mind, and end up eating dinner late and frazzeled.

TO - BE SCENARIO
After documenting the user's as-is experience, I then created an ideal future experience that solves the painpoints that emerged in the as-is flow.




STATEMENTS OF INTENT AND LOW FIDELITY WIREFRAMES

1 Lauren, a reorcurring HelloFresh customer, is able to enjoy the experience of cooking with another person, and sharing that time together.




2 Lauren and Jim, her spouse, are able to accurately estimate the amount of time a recipe will take to cook with two cooks.




USER TESTING
I did 3 rounds of user testing, and each round led to valuable insights that improved the design.



Feedback from user testing
Share recipe with other people that have the app
The general recipe instructions are hard to read in paragraph form
Tabbing interaction between Chef 1 and Chef 2 instructions would clash with swiping in between steps
When Chef 2 opens the link, it takes he/she to Chef 2 tab in Step 1


Solution

HIGH FIDELITY ITERATIONS
FINAL DESIGNS

HIGH FIDELITY ITERATIONS

Once the user experience was nailed down, I did several iterations of visual design to better execute my idea at a high fidelity. I experimented with tabs, a key, text messenger style format, I analyzed how the recipe content could be broken up, and worked on minor tweeks to content design.





FINAL DESIGNS

Through this process I arrived at my final design that not only work for 2 cooks but also improves the cooking experience for one person as well. I came to the conclusion that yes, this problem is an important one that needs to be solved, but it's solution can be minimally invasive and not have to be an entirely separate use case. Instead it can be more streamlined into the already existing cooking experience within the HelloFresh app. More specifically I realized that overall there are 2 natural roles in the process: A cooking role, and a prepping role. So naturally my final designs manifested from this idea. While this is just the beginning design work of solving this problem, I do believe with more analysis and user research my designs would enable a more enjoyable cooking experience!
